Understanding cooking terms

Written by Aung Bhone Pyae Thaw

Cooking terms refer to the specific techniques used in cooking. Understanding these terms is essential for following recipes and achieving the best flavors and textures. There are many cooking terms and here is a list of the most common cooking terms.

Basic Cooking Methods

  1. Sauté: Cooking food quickly in a small amount of oil or fat over medium-high heat.
  2. Boil: Cooking liquid is heated until it reaches 100 C and bubbles vigorously.
  3. Simmer: Cooking at a temperature just below boiling, where small bubbles form and rise gently.
  4. Bake: Cooking food in an oven using dry heat.
  5. Roast: Similar to baking but often involves higher heat for meats and vegetables.
  6. Fry: Cooking food in hot fat or oil, either fully submerged (deep-frying) or partially (pan-frying)
  7. Grilling: Grilling cooks food over direct heat, creating a smoky flavor and crispy exterior. It's commonly used for meats, vegetables, and fish, offering quick and flavorful meals.
  8. Steam: To cook food using steam, typically over boiling water.
  9. Poach: To cook food gently in simmering liquid, often used for eggs or fish.

Cutting & Preparation Techniques

  1. Dice: Cutting food into small, even cubes.
  2. Julienne: Cutting food into thin, matchstick-sized strips.
  3. Chop: Cutting food into irregular pieces, typically larger then diced pieces.
  4. Mince: To finely chop food, usually meat or vegetables.
  5. Grate: To shred food into small pieces, typically using a grater.

Flavoring and Enhancing

  1. Marinate: To soak food in a seasoned liquid mixture before cooking to enhance flavor and tenderness.
  2. Deglaze: To add liquid to a pan to loosen the flavorful bits of food left after sautéing or roasting.

Is that all?

Of course not! There's still a lot more cooking terms that I haven't mentioned. As I've said, this is just the basics of it. If your dream is to become a master chef, there are many other sources where you can find more complicated and sophisticated cooking terms. Thanks for reading 'til the end!
