How to pick fresh ingredients and what to avoid when choosing ingredients

Written by Aung Bhone Pyae Thaw

Cooking doesn't always depend on cooking skills, it also depends on the ingredients and materials that the cooks use. That's why this tips and techniques is about how to pick fresh ingredients and what to avoid when choosing them.

How to choose fresh ingredients

  1. Fruits: Look for vibrant colors, firm textures, and avoid bruises or soft spots.
  2. Vegetables: Choose crisp, brightly colored vegetables without wilting or yellowing.
  3. Meat: Select meat that is bright red or pink with no grey or brown spots; it should smell fresh, not sour.
  4. Fish: Look for shiny skin, clear eyes, and a mild ocean smell.
  5. Dairy: Check the expiration date and ensure itâ€™s properly refrigerated. Avoid milk or yoghurt with a sour smell or lumpy texture.
  6. Eggs: Test freshness by placing them in water; fresh eggs sink, while older ones float.
  7. Packaged Foods: Check the expiration date and avoid damaged or bulging packaging.

How can fresh ingredients impact your dishes?

Fresh ingredients enhance dishes by providing richer flavours, vibrant colours, and better textures. They are packed with nutrients, making meals healthier and more enjoyable. Additionally, fresh produce and proteins cook evenly and add a natural aroma that elevates the overall dining experience. Using fresh ingredients ensures high-quality and visually appealing results every time.
