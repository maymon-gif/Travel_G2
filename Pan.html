How to Use a Pan in Different Ways

Essential cooking techniques for using your pan!

Searing

Searing is great for creating a crispy, flavorful crust on meats like steak or fish. Heat your pan until it's very hot, then cook the meat on each side until a golden-brown crust forms.

Sautéing

Sautéing involves cooking food quickly over medium-high heat with a little oil or butter. It’s perfect for vegetables and meats like chicken. Stir frequently to avoid burning.

Frying

Frying gives your food a crispy, golden texture. Heat enough oil in your pan to submerge the food partially or completely. Be sure to maintain the right temperature to avoid soggy results.

Simmering & Poaching

Simmering involves cooking food gently in hot, not boiling, liquid. Poaching is similar but at an even lower temperature. Use these techniques for delicate items like eggs or fish.

Stir-frying

Stir-frying is perfect for quick, high-heat cooking of small pieces of food. Toss ingredients in a hot pan with oil for a delicious stir-fry, keeping everything moving to avoid burning.

Grilling (Grill Pan)

Grill pans mimic the effects of outdoor grilling indoors. Use them to create grill marks on meat and vegetables for that smoky flavor, especially if you don’t have an outdoor grill.

Braising

Braising involves cooking meat slowly in a small amount of liquid. This technique makes tougher cuts of meat tender and flavorful by cooking them low and slow.

Sauce Production

Pan sauces are easy to make by using the leftover bits (fond) in the pan after cooking meat. Add liquid like wine or stock, and let it reduce for a rich, flavorful sauce.

Pancakes/Waffles

Cook pancakes or waffles in a heated pan for that perfect golden-brown color. Make sure to grease the pan with butter or oil for a non-stick surface.
